Yongyi Yang (杨永祎)

Yongyi is a third-year Ph.D. student at the University of Michigan, advised by Prof. Wei Hu. He had an internship at NTT Research at Harvard under the advisement of Dr. Hidenori Tanaka, with whom he continues collaborating closely. His research focuses on understanding the foundations and principles of deep learning, spanning research areas such as deep learning theory, science of deep learning, and mechanistic interpretability. Besides, he is also broadly interested in many other exciting research topics of computer science, including quantization and graph neural networks.

Yongyi received his Bachelor of Science from Fudan university, under the supervision of Prof. Xipeng Qiu. He also had an internship at Amazon Shanghai AI Lab and has his fortune to be advised by Dr. David Wipf and Prof. Zengfeng Huang.

Besides academic research, Yongyi also harbors a passion in mathematics, Chinese classical literature and XiaoXue. Feel free to contact if you share the same interests.
